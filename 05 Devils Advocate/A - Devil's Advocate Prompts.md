# Devil's Advocate SOP — AeroSTAR Boeing Challenge

> Purpose: Stress-test every idea before a judge, airline, or Boeing reviewer does.

## Mission

Your role is **not** to improve ideas first. Your role is to **break them**. Assume every concept is flawed until it survives rigorous criticism.

---

# Universal Devil's Advocate Prompt

```
You are a hostile but fair review panel made up of:

- Airline CFO
- Airport Ground Operations Manager
- Boeing Sustainability Director
- Cabin Crew Manager
- Passenger Experience Expert
- Behavioral Economist
- Competition Judge

Your goal is to find every possible weakness.

Do NOT generate new ideas until every weakness has been identified.

For each idea, evaluate:

1. Operational feasibility
2. Cost implications
3. Passenger friction
4. Staff acceptance
5. Scalability
6. Behavioral effectiveness
7. Sustainability impact
8. Boeing alignment
9. SDG 12 alignment
10. SDG 13 alignment

Specifically reject ideas that:
- Require new aircraft hardware
- Require structural aircraft modifications
- Require standalone mobile apps
- Increase boarding time
- Increase aircraft turnaround time
- Increase cabin crew workload without compensation
- Depend on unrealistic passenger behavior
- Lack measurable outcomes

Output format:

## Executive Verdict
Pass / Revise / Reject

## Fatal Flaws
(List the biggest issues.)

## Hidden Risks
(Problems likely to appear during deployment.)

## Judge Questions
(Questions the panel would ask.)

## Strongest Counterarguments
(Why someone would reject this.)

## How To Improve
(Only after criticism.)

## Final Score
Feasibility: /10
Innovation: /10
Operational Integration: /10
Behavioral Science: /10
Sustainability: /10
Competition Readiness: /10
Overall: /100

Be brutally honest.
```

---

# Pre-Mortem Prompt

Use before investing time in an idea.

```
Assume this project completely failed at the AeroSTAR Boeing Finals.

Explain exactly why.

Identify:
- Operational mistakes
- Weak assumptions
- Judge objections
- Passenger objections
- Airline objections
- Missing evidence
- Poor storytelling
- Weak SDG links

Then rank the top five reasons for failure.
```

---

# Airline Executive Review

```
Review this proposal only as an airline executive.

Your priorities:

1. Profitability
2. Operational efficiency
3. Brand reputation
4. Passenger satisfaction
5. Regulatory compliance

Ignore creativity.

Would you approve funding?

Why or why not?
```

---

# Boeing Judge Review

```
Review this proposal exactly like a Boeing judge.

Score:

Innovation
Feasibility
Systems Thinking
Environmental Impact
Evidence
Presentation Potential

Identify what would prevent this proposal from reaching the regional camp.

Be specific and uncompromising.
```

---

# Red Team Checklist

Before declaring any concept "finished," answer YES to all:

- Uses no new aircraft hardware
- Requires no new mobile app
- Fits existing airline workflows
- Adds zero boarding delay
- Supported by evidence
- Clear SDG 12 impact
- Clear SDG 13 impact
- Behavior change is measurable
- Easy for passengers
- Easy for staff
- Financially realistic
- Judges can understand in under 30 seconds

If any answer is NO, revise the concept before moving forward.

---

# Golden Rule

"The goal is not to prove our idea is good. The goal is to discover why it might fail before the judges do."
