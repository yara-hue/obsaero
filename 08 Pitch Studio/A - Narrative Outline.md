# Pitch Narrative Architecture

> **Purpose:** The emotional and structural story arc for the pitch.
> **When to use:** Before writing slides — outline the narrative flow here.

### FILE PURPOSE
The emotional and structural outline of our presentation, built around the Hero's Journey framework.

**How to use:** Draft the story arc here before writing individual slides. Map out where the hook is, where the data drops, and where the SDG impact is revealed.

---

## Hero's Journey Arc

### Hook (0:00 - 0:30)
<span style="color: var(--text-faint);">Open with a surprising fact, story, or question.</span>
- <span style="color: var(--text-faint);">e.g. Every year, 6 million kilograms of waste are generated in airplane cabins — and 80% of it goes to landfill.</span>

### Problem (0:30 - 1:30)
<span style="color: var(--text-faint);">Define the problem in human terms.</span>
- <span style="color: var(--text-faint);">e.g. Passengers want to recycle, but they cannot — the bins are confusing, unlabeled, and crew do not have time to explain.</span>

### Solution Reveal (1:30 - 3:00)
<span style="color: var(--text-faint);">Introduce the concept as the answer.</span>
- <span style="color: var(--text-faint);">e.g. What if every seat became a smart sorting station?</span>

### Data Drop (3:00 - 4:00)
<span style="color: var(--text-faint);">Hit them with numbers.</span>
- <span style="color: var(--text-faint);">e.g. Survey results, waste reduction percentages, cost savings</span>

### SDG Impact Reveal (4:00 - 4:30)
<span style="color: var(--text-faint);">Connect to the bigger picture.</span>
- <span style="color: var(--text-faint);">e.g. This directly addresses SDG 12 and SDG 13 — and here is how.</span>

### Call to Action (4:30 - 5:00)
<span style="color: var(--text-faint);">Close with urgency.</span>
- <span style="color: var(--text-faint);">e.g. Airlines are ready. Passengers are ready. The only thing missing is this system.</span>
